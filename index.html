SCFHS Prometric Mock Exam

Phase 1 – Questions 1 to 30

1. On orientation day, a nurse educator said to a new nurse "our hospital provides the highest level of specialized healthcare in an integrated educational and research setting" which of the following aspects of the hospital's strategic plan is indicated by this statement?

Correct Answer: A – Vision
Why (SCFHS): Vision describes the aspirational future direction of the organization.
Distractors:
  • B – Policy: NCLEX trap; policies are rules, not aspirations.
  • C – Values: STR; values describe beliefs, not direction.
  • D – Mission: Misleading; mission focuses on current operations.

2. A head nurse of an intensive care unit wrote a report about the needs of her department and gave it to the nursing supervisor. Which of the following types of communication is this?

Correct Answer: A – Upward
Why: Information flows from subordinate to higher authority.
Distractors:
  • B – Horizontal: Same level communication.
  • C – Diagonal: Between different departments.
  • D – Downward: Supervisor to staff.

3. A head nurse of a coronary care unit delegated staff scheduling to a senior nurse in that unit. Which of the following steps must the head nurse implement before delegating tasks?

Correct Answer: D – Make sure hospital policies for delegating tasks
Why: Legal and institutional guidelines must be verified before delegation.
Distractors:
  • A – Negotiation: Comes later.
  • B – Signature: Not required for delegation.
  • C – Explanation: Important but not the first legal step.

4. A nurse is planning care for a patient with chronic kidney disease. Which of the following nursing actions best reflects the principle of patient-centered care?

Correct Answer: B – Including the patient in care decisions
Why: Patient-centered care emphasizes the involvement of patients in planning and decision-making.
Distractors:
  • A – Following orders only: Task-focused, not patient-centered.
  • C – Delegating education: Partial responsibility; patient involvement is key.
  • D – Documentation: Important for safety but not direct patient-centered action.

5. A nurse observes a colleague using incorrect hand hygiene technique. Which of the following is the best immediate action?

Correct Answer: C – Politely correct the colleague immediately
Why: Immediate correction prevents infection risk and promotes safe practice.
Distractors:
  • A – Ignore: Unsafe and unprofessional.
  • B – Report later: Delay may risk patient safety.
  • D – Document: Documentation does not prevent harm.

6. Which of the following statements best represents a nurse’s accountability in professional practice?

Correct Answer: B – Being responsible for personal actions and decisions
Why: Accountability requires accepting responsibility for professional actions.
Distractors:
  • A – Blindly following orders: Does not reflect accountability.
  • C – Delegating high-risk tasks: Accountability remains with the delegator.
  • D – Avoiding errors: Unrealistic and not sufficient alone.

7. A patient refuses a blood transfusion for religious reasons. Which ethical principle is the nurse upholding by respecting this decision?

Correct Answer: C – Autonomy
Why: Autonomy respects a patient’s right to make informed decisions about their own care.
Distractors:
  • A – Beneficence: Focuses on doing good, not respecting refusal.
  • B – Justice: Fair treatment, unrelated here.
  • D – Nonmaleficence: Avoiding harm, not directly related to choice.

8. In delegating tasks, which of the following is the nurse’s legal and ethical responsibility?

Correct Answer: A – Ensure tasks are within delegatee’s competence
Why: Delegation must match knowledge, skill, and licensure.
Distractors:
  • B – Overdelegation: Unsafe and illegal.
  • C – No supervision: Neglects accountability.
  • D – No verification: Violates safe practice standards.

9. A nurse provides care based on evidence from clinical research. Which type of nursing practice does this exemplify?

Correct Answer: B – Evidence-based practice
Why: Care decisions are guided by research evidence.
Distractors:
  • A – Traditional: Based on habit or custom.
  • C – Intuitive: Based on personal judgment without evidence.
  • D – Policy-driven: Based on institutional rules, not research.

10. A nurse is teaching a patient about insulin administration. Which teaching method best ensures patient understanding?

Correct Answer: B – Demonstration and return demonstration
Why: Active demonstration with patient return ensures comprehension and skill acquisition.
Distractors:
  • A – Lecture: Passive; less retention.
  • C – Handout only: May not ensure skill mastery.
  • D – Reading silently: No verification of understanding.

11. A nurse is caring for a patient with a new prescription for an anticoagulant. Which is the most important action before administering the medication?

Correct Answer: A – Assess lab values for bleeding risk
Why: Anticoagulants can cause bleeding; lab checks are essential prior to administration.
Distractors:
  • B – Dietary advice: Important but secondary.
  • C – Administer immediately: Unsafe without assessment.
  • D – Documentation: Follows assessment, not first action.

12. A nurse is evaluating a patient’s pain management plan. Which of the following demonstrates effective evaluation?

Correct Answer: B – Asking patient if pain has improved
Why: Evaluation focuses on outcomes and patient perception.
Distractors:
  • A – Documentation: Records actions but not effectiveness.
  • C – Vital signs: Indirect indicator only.
  • D – Scheduling: Administrative, not evaluative.

13. A patient develops sudden shortness of breath and chest pain. Which nursing action has priority?

Correct Answer: B – Assess vital signs and oxygen saturation
Why: Immediate assessment is critical for airway, breathing, and circulation.
Distractors:
  • A – Notify family: Secondary priority.
  • C – Documentation: Important but not immediate.
  • D – Administer scheduled meds: Not urgent in crisis.

14. When teaching a patient with diabetes about insulin injection, which strategy promotes the best retention?

Correct Answer: C – Demonstration with return demonstration
Why: Active participation ensures skill mastery.
Distractors:
  • A – Demo only: Passive, may not verify understanding.
  • B – Written only: Insufficient for psychomotor skills.
  • D – Lecture: Passive, less retention.

15. Which communication technique is most appropriate when giving constructive feedback to a colleague?

Correct Answer: B – In private, with specific examples
Why: Private, specific feedback improves performance and maintains trust.
Distractors:
  • A – Public: Embarrassing and counterproductive.
  • C – Email: Impersonal, lacks clarity.
  • D – Avoid: Does not support quality care.

16. A nurse observes a medication error. Which action is correct?

Correct Answer: B – Report immediately according to policy
Why: Prompt reporting ensures patient safety and legal compliance.
Distractors:
  • A – Ignore: Unsafe and unprofessional.
  • C – Discuss only: Insufficient for accountability.
  • D – Delay: Risk to patient.

17. Which action demonstrates ethical beneficence?

Correct Answer: A – Providing pain medication to relieve suffering
Why: Beneficence is the ethical principle of doing good.
Distractors:
  • B – Autonomy: Respecting choice, not beneficence.
  • C – Policy: Compliance, not ethical action per se.
  • D – Delegation to unlicensed: Unsafe, unethical.

18. Which nursing action demonstrates accountability?

Correct Answer: A – Refusing to perform tasks beyond scope
Why: Accountability involves responsibility for safe, competent care.
Distractors:
  • B – Delegating all: Abdicates responsibility.
  • C – Ignoring documentation: Fails accountability.
  • D – Avoiding care: Neglects professional responsibility.

19. Which of the following best illustrates patient advocacy?

Correct Answer: A – Ensuring the patient understands treatment options
Why: Advocacy involves protecting patient rights and ensuring informed decisions.
Distractors:
  • B – Efficiency: Not advocacy.
  • C – Delegation: Neutral, not advocacy.
  • D – Avoiding questions: Violates advocacy principles.

20. Which principle ensures fairness in allocation of limited nursing resources?

Correct Answer: A – Justice
Why: Justice refers to fair treatment and resource allocation.
Distractors:
  • B – Autonomy: Patient choice.
  • C – Beneficence: Doing good.
  • D – Fidelity: Keeping promises.
